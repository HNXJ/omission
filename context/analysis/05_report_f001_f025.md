# Omission Project: Comprehensive Analysis Report (Figures 1-25)

This report provides a detailed overview of the current analytical suite (Phase 1-2) for the Omission project. Each figure is accompanied by its methodology, expected results, and functional role in the hierarchy.

## Figure 1: Theory Schematic (`f001_theory`)
- **Goal**: Illustrate the predictive routing model.
- **Analysis**: Conceptual diagram of Layer 2/3 (Feedforward/Gamma/PE) vs. Layer 5/6 (Feedback/Alpha-Beta/Predictions).
- **Results**: High-level block diagram with directed routing arrows.
- **Significance**: Sets the theoretical stage for the "Omission" as the pure test of internal predictions.

## Figure 2: Experimental Design (`f002_psth`)
- **Goal**: Summarize the task (AAAB vs AXAB) and recording setup.
- **Analysis**: Synthetic timeline of 4-stimulus sequences and a CSD (Current Source Density) dipole map for layer assignment.
- **Results**: Clear timeline of stimulus/omission windows and a color-coded depth map showing sinks/sources.
- **Significance**: Foundations of the temporal and spatial (laminar) context.

## Figure 3: Area-Averaged PSTH (`f003_surprise`)
- **Goal**: Compare firing rates during Standard vs. Omission.
- **Analysis**: Area-level population average of single-unit activity aligned to stimulus/omission onset.
- **Results**: Standard (AAAB) shows stimulus-driven peaks; Omission (AXAB) shows internally-driven "Surprise" peaks (e.g., in V4, PFC).
- **Significance**: Empirical proof of "Omission" responses across the 11-area hierarchy.

## Figure 4: Population State-Space (`f004_coding`)
- **Goal**: Visualize the neural manifold divergence.
- **Analysis**: 3D PCA on population PSTHs (-500ms to 2000ms from P1).
- **Results**: Divergent trajectories for Standard and Omission states, particularly evident in higher-order areas.
- **Significance**: Demonstrates that omission "surprise" is a distinct global state shift.

## Figure 5: Time-Frequency Representation (`f005_tfr`)
- **Goal**: Map spectral power over time.
- **Analysis**: Wavelet/Multitaper TFR of LFP signals aligned to Omission.
- **Results**: Evoked Gamma bursts during stimulus; persistent/ramping Beta bursts during omission.
- **Significance**: Identifies the frequency bands carrying internal predictions vs. sensory errors.

## Figure 6: Band-Specific Dynamics (`f006_band_power`)
- **Goal**: Quantify temporal power profiles in dB.
- **Analysis**: Filtering (Theta, Alpha, Beta, Gamma) and Hilbert envelope extraction.
- **Results**: Relative power (dB) showing Alpha/Beta ramping before omission and Gamma suppression during omission.
- **Significance**: Detailed temporal tracking of oscillatory "Predictive Routing" mechanisms.

## Figure 7: Spike-Field Coherence (`f007_sfc`)
- **Goal**: Measure neural synchronization (PLV).
- **Analysis**: Phase-Locking Value (PLV) spectrum between single units and local LFP, subsampling-corrected for spike count.
- **Results**: S+ (Stimulus) units lock to Gamma; O+ (Omission) units lock to Beta.
- **Significance**: Links unit-level "surprise" to specific global oscillatory rhythms.

## Figure 8: Spectral Coordination (`f008_coordination`)
- **Goal**: Map cross-area harmonic coupling.
- **Analysis**: Inter-area envelope correlations of band-pass filtered LFPs.
- **Results**: 11x11 Pearson correlation matrices showing Beta-band "harmony" across the hierarchy during omissions.
- **Significance**: Proves that omission signals are distributed across the V1-PFC axis.

## Figure 9: Individual Neuron SFC (`f009_individual_sfc`)
- **Goal**: Show the diversity of single-neuron coupling.
- **Analysis**: Individual unit PLV spectra for the top 20 O+ and S+ neurons per area.
- **Results**: Spaghetti plots showing unit-level locking preferences.
- **Significance**: Demonstrates consistency (or heterogeneity) of the "Predictive Routing" logic at the unit level.

## Figure 10: SFC Delta Contrast (`f010_sfc_delta`)
- **Goal**: Isolate the "Surprise" shift in synchronization.
- **Analysis**: Contrast (Omission PLV - Standard PLV) across the spectrum.
- **Results**: Positive peaks in Beta (ΔPLV > 0) and negative peaks in Gamma (ΔPLV < 0) during omissions.
- **Significance**: The "Fingerprint of Surprise"—synchronization shifts from FF (Gamma) to FB (Beta).

## Figure 11: Laminar Routing (`f011_laminar`)
- **Goal**: Localize spectral effects to cortical layers.
- **Analysis**: Depth-resolved spectral power (Beta vs Gamma) during the omission window.
- **Results**: Gamma peaks in superficial layers (L2/3); Beta peaks in deep layers (L5/6).
- **Significance**: Structural validation of the Predictive Routing theory.

## Figure 12: Connectivity Matrix (`f012_mi_matrix`)
- **Goal**: Map the full inter-area functional connectome.
- **Analysis**: Mutual Information (MI) between Unit-Area spikes and LFP-Area bands (11x11).
- **Results**: MI matrices (Theta, Alpha, Beta, Gamma) across 9 temporal frames.
- **Significance**: First comprehensive map of directed information flow in the V1-PFC hierarchy during omissions.

## Figure 13: Connectivity Graphs (`f013_connectivity_graph`)
- **Goal**: Visualize the network topology.
- **Analysis**: Directed graphs on the hierarchy where edges represent top-percentile MI.
- **Results**: Hierarchy graphs (17 frames) showing the emergence of top-down Beta networks.
- **Significance**: Intuitive visualization of "Internal Model" recruitment.

## Figure 14: Connectivity Delta (`f014_connectivity_delta`)
- **Goal**: Highlight the "Surprise" network shift.
- **Analysis**: MI contrast (AXAB - AAAB) across the 11x11 matrix.
- **Results**: RdBu heatmaps showing which pathways are "activated" by the omission of a stimulus.
- **Significance**: Pinpoints the specific pathways (e.g., PFC -> V4) involved in prediction error routing.

## Figure 15: Global Dynamics Summary (`f015_global_dynamics`)
- **Goal**: Track total network integration over time.
- **Analysis**: Mean inter-area MI (average of the 11x11 matrix) across the 17 temporal frames.
- **Results**: Clear peaks in network-wide MI during stimulus/omission windows.
- **Significance**: Measures the "Global Workspace" engagement during the task.

## Figure 16: Impedance Profiles (`f016_impedance_profiles`)
- **Goal**: Characterize the electrode-tissue interface.
- **Analysis**: Complex Impedance Tensor ($Z_{eff}$) estimated from LFP and MUAe using JAX.
- **Results**: Depth-Frequency maps of Magnitude and Phase ($∠Z_{eff}$).
- **Significance**: Ensures physical signal integrity and characterizes local conductivity/permittivity.

## Figure 17: Prediction Error Scaling (`f017_prediction_errors`)
- **Goal**: Quantify Surprise scaling with Stimulus sequence position.
- **Analysis**: Scaling of PE-unit magnitude with number of preceding A's.
- **Results**: Linear/Sigmoidal scaling of surprise magnitude.
- **Significance**: Proves the "Predictive" nature of the responses.

## Figure 18: Ghost Signal Transients (`f018_ghost_signals`)
- **Goal**: Detect internally generated sensory-like transients.
- **Analysis**: Stimulus-specific "Ghost" signals during omissions using template matching.
- **Results**: Transient bursts during omissions that share the same subspace as the real stimulus.
- **Significance**: Empirical evidence for "Mental Imagery" or internal template activation.

## Figure 19: Phase-Amplitude Coupling (`f019_pac_analysis`)
- **Goal**: Measure cross-frequency integration.
- **Analysis**: PAC (Modulation Index) between low-frequency phases and high-frequency amplitudes.
- **Results**: Theta-Gamma and Beta-Gamma coupling shifts during omission.
- **Significance**: Mechanistic link for how global predictions gate local gamma bursts.

## Figure 20: Effective Connectivity (`f020_effective_connectivity`)
- **Goal**: Quantify directed causal flow.
- **Analysis**: Granger Causality or Directed MI between area pairs.
- **Results**: Directed flow from PFC to V1 in Beta band during omissions.
- **Significance**: Direct proof of feedback-driven information routing.

## Figure 21: Pupil Decoding (`f021_pupil_decoding`)
- **Goal**: Link neural surprise to autonomic state.
- **Analysis**: Population decoding of pupil diameter from neural activity.
- **Results**: Neural activity in PFC/V4 predicts pupil dilation peaks following surprise.
- **Significance**: Behavioral/Physiological relevance of the "Surprise" signal.

## Figure 22: PCA Trajectories (`f022_dimensionality_reduction`)
- **Goal**: Higher-dimensional state tracking.
- **Analysis**: GPFA/PCA trajectories for individual conditions (A vs B vs R).
- **Results**: 3D manifolds showing trial-to-trial variability and state clustering.
- **Significance**: Robustness of condition representations.

## Figure 23: Spectral Fingerprints (`f023_spectral_fingerprints`)
- **Goal**: Define area-specific spectral identity.
- **Analysis**: Baseline-normalized PSD (Power Spectral Density) for each area.
- **Results**: Area-specific peaks (e.g., V1=Gamma, PFC=Theta/Beta).
- **Significance**: Canonical reference for hierarchy location.

## Figure 24: Fano Factor Dynamics (`f024_fano_factor`)
- **Goal**: Track neural variability quenching.
- **Analysis**: Sliding window (50ms) Fano Factor ($Var/Mean$) over trials.
- **Results**: Reduction in FF during stimulus and predictive omission windows.
- **Significance**: Measures the "precision" of internal predictions.

## Figure 25: Condition State Decoding (`f025_state_decoding`)
- **Goal**: Measure the discriminability of the Standard/Omission state.
- **Analysis**: Logistic Regression population decoder on 100ms sliding windows.
- **Results**: Accuracy rises to ~1.0 during surprise, lagging stimulus-driven accuracy.
- **Significance**: Quantifies the "Latency of Surprise" across the hierarchy.
