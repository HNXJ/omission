# Neuroscience Writing: Foundational IMRAD Structure

Guides the high-level organization of biophysical and systems neuroscience papers.

## 1. The Narrative Arc
- **Title**: Must balance specific findings (e.g., "PV reduction") with functional impact (e.g., "Weakened Gamma").
- **Abstract**: The "miniature paper"—context, gap, hypothesis, method, finding, significance.
- **Introduction**: The funnel—broad disease context $\to$ circuit mechanics $\to$ specific hypothesis.
- **Methods**: The "Recipe"—detailed enough for 100% reproduction of the model and analysis.
- **Results**: The "Evidence"—unbiased presentation of data through figures and text.
- **Discussion**: The "Synthesis"—integrating findings back into the global field and clinical context.

## 2. Formatting Standards
- **Font**: Prefer high-legibility sans-serif (e.g., Helvetica/Arial) for figures.
- **Style**: Use "Active Voice" for results ("We found that...") and "Passive Voice" for methods ("Cells were simulated...").
- **Tense**: Present tense for general facts; Past tense for your specific study results.
