# Schizophrenia Theory Evaluation (ScZ-51) -- Glossary Reference

This document defines the 51 biophysical factors used to evaluate theories of Schizophrenia circuit deficits, organized into three 17-factor hypothesis groups.

---

## H1: GABAergic Interneuron Deficit (IDs 1--17)
Focuses on the primary local microcircuit breakdown and inhibitory pacing failure.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 1 | PV mRNA Reduction | Significant decrease in Parvalbumin mRNA expression in L2/3. | Neurobiology |
| 2 | GAD67 Deficit | Reduction in the 67kDa isoform of glutamic acid decarboxylase. | Neurobiology |
| 3 | Fast-Spiking Failure | Inability of PV interneurons to maintain high-frequency pacing. | Modeling |
| 4 | SST mRNA Reduction | Decrease in Somatostatin expression in superficial layers. | Neurobiology |
| 5 | Perisomatic Shunting Gain | Altered effectiveness of PV-mediated shunting at the soma. | Biophysical |
| 6 | Dendritic Gating Failure | Weakened SST-mediated inhibition of distal dendritic feedback. | Biophysical |
| 7 | CCK-Interneuron Dysregulation | Altered activity of Cholecystokinin-positive basket cells. | Qualitative |
| 8 | L3 PV Density Shift | Anatomical reduction of PV+ cell counts specifically in Layer 3. | Anatomical |
| 9 | GAT-1 Downregulation | Reduced GABA transporter-1 expression at inhibitory terminals. | Quantitative |
| 10 | GABA-A $\alpha$1 subunit shift | Compensatory changes in postsynaptic receptor composition. | Neuropharma |
| 11 | Synchrony Pacing Deficit | Failure of interneuron networks to drive coherent oscillations. | Modeling |
| 12 | Chloride Polarity Shift | KCC2/NKCC1 imbalance causing GABAergic depolarization. | Neurobiology |
| 13 | E/I Ratio Volatility | Increased fluctuations in local excitation/inhibition balance. | Functional |
| 14 | PING/ING Disruption | Disrupted pyramidal-interneuron or interneuron-interneuron gamma. | Modeling |
| 15 | PV-Pyr Synaptic Weakening | Reduced maximal conductance ($g_{max}$) of PV $\to$ E synapses. | Biophysical |
| 16 | Basket Cell Pacing Consistency | Inconsistency in rhythmic firing of perisomatic basket cells. | Functional |
| 17 | Interneuron Recruitment Delay | Systematic delay in the activation of I-units during drive. | Modeling |

## H2: Top-down Feedback Dysfunction (IDs 18--34)
Focuses on hierarchical disconnection and the failure of internal models to guide sensory processing.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 18 | MMN Blunting | Reduced amplitude of Mismatch Negativity ERP to oddballs. | Neurophys |
| 19 | Enhanced Baseline Beta | Elevated 15-25Hz power during rest or pre-stimulus. | Neurophys |
| 20 | FB-to-L1 Drive Failure | Weakened top-down feedback to Layer 1 apical dendrites. | Modeling |
| 21 | FB-to-SST Gain modulation | Disrupted top-down control of SST-mediated inhibition. | Modeling |
| 22 | Deep-Layer Spine Loss | Significant reduction in dendritic spines on L5/6 pyramidal cells. | Anatomical |
| 23 | Prediction Error Attenuation | Reduced neural differentiation between expected and unexpected. | Functional |
| 24 | Hierarchical Prediction Precision | Reduced weighting of top-down internal models (beliefs). | Modeling |
| 25 | Sensory Gating Failure | Failure to filter redundant information (P50 deficit). | Neurophys |
| 26 | NMDAR Hypofunction (Deep) | Specifically targeting feedback integration sites in deep layers. | Neurobiology |
| 27 | Feedback-Dendritic Disconnection | Loss of recurrent connectivity between hierarchical levels. | Structural |
| 28 | Supragranular Prediction Error | Pathological accumulation of PE signals in L2/3. | Modeling |
| 29 | Long-Range Phase Coherence | Reduced synchronization between PFC and sensory areas. | Neurophys |
| 30 | Alpha-Beta Feedback Gating | Disrupted use of low-freqs to gate feedforward gamma. | Modeling |
| 31 | FB-mediated E/I Balancing | Failure of top-down signals to stabilize local E/I ratios. | Functional |
| 32 | Hierarchical Latency Shift | Abnormal delays in the propagation of feedback signals. | Neurophys |
| 33 | Context-Dependent Gain | Failure to adjust unit gain based on hierarchical context. | Modeling |
| 34 | Predictive Routing Failure | Breakdown of the Selective Routing of prediction errors. | Functional |

## H3: Ubiquity of Deficits (IDs 35--51)
Focuses on the brain-wide consistency and the global scaling of the pathology.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 35 | White Matter Disintegrity | Global disruption of long-range axonal tracts. | Neurobiology |
| 36 | BDNF Expression Deficit | Global reduction in BDNF levels affecting brain-wide plasticity. | Neurobiology |
| 37 | Area-Invariant Gamma Drop | Detection of weakened gamma power across all cortical pillars. | Neurophys |
| 38 | Global 1/f Slope Flattening | Brain-wide reduction in the aperiodic power spectrum slope. | Neurophys |
| 39 | Cross-modal MMN Deficit | Preservation of PE signaling failure across visual and auditory. | Functional |
| 40 | Hierarchical Deficit Scaling ($H_i$) | Pathology increases as a function of depth in the hierarchy. | Modeling |
| 41 | Global Aperiodic Volatility | Increased fluctuations in background noise across all areas. | Modeling |
| 42 | Delta/Theta Baseline Surge | Global increase in low-frequency power during wakefulness. | Neurophys |
| 43 | Widespread GAD67 Downregulation | Detection of GABA synthesis deficits across the entire neocortex. | Neurobiology |
| 44 | Brain-wide Connectivity Decay | Reduction in the number of stable long-range connections. | Structural |
| 45 | Global Synchrony Decay | Decay of coherent population oscillations at large spatial scales. | Functional |
| 46 | Cross-Area Mutual Information | Reduced information transfer between distant brain regions. | Modeling |
| 47 | Ubiquitous NMDAR Hypofunction | Global presence of NMDA receptor deficits on PV+ cells. | Neurobiology |
| 48 | Universal Canonical Motif | Pathology preserves the basic CMC structure across all areas. | Qualitative |
| 49 | Global Neural Quenching Failure | Brain-wide inability to reduce variance upon stimulus onset. | Neurophys |
| 50 | Temporal Stability of Deficits | Consistent presence of pathology throughout chronic stages. | Functional |
| 51 | Hierarchical Hub Collapse | Specifically targeting high-degree hubs (e.g. PFC, Thalamus). | Modeling |
