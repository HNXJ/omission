# Electromagnetic Oscillations & Brain Signals (EMO-36) -- Glossary Reference

This document defines the 36 biophysical factors used to evaluate theories of brain oscillations and their relationship with spiking activity, organized into three 12-factor hypothesis groups.

---

## H1: Functional Role of Oscillations (IDs 1--12)
Evaluates if oscillations are causal functional units or simply epiphenomena.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Scale |
|----|-------------|------------|-------|
| 1 | Causal Pacing | Oscillations actively gate and coordinate neuronal firing timing. | -1 to 1 |
| 2 | Information Routing | Specific frequencies (e.g. Gamma) route signals across cortical areas. | -1 to 1 |
| 3 | Temporal Binding | Synchrony binds disparate sensory features into unified objects. | -1 to 1 |
| 4 | Working Memory Buffering | Theta-Gamma coupling maintains discrete items in active memory. | -1 to 1 |
| 5 | Phase-Reset Signaling | Event-related phase resets convey high-precision sensory timing. | -1 to 1 |
| 6 | Metabolic Efficiency | Oscillations optimize the energy cost of large-scale spike transmission. | -1 to 1 |
| 7 | Communication-through-Coherence | Coherent phases between areas allow for maximum synaptic gain. | -1 to 1 |
| 8 | Neural Manifold Alignment | Oscillations align population activity with empirical latent subspaces. | -1 to 1 |
| 9 | Prediction Error Signaling | High-frequency motifs (Gamma) convey surprise in hierarchical PC. | -1 to 1 |
| 10 | Attentional Gain Gating | Top-down oscillations modulate the gain of local sensory units. | -1 to 1 |
| 11 | Dimensionality Compression | Fields project high-dimensional spiking onto low-dimensional manifolds. | -1 to 1 |
| 12 | Network State Stability | Oscillatory regimes stabilize network dynamics against perturbations. | -1 to 1 |

## H2: Independence from Spiking (IDs 13--24)
Evaluates the degree to which LFP/MEG signals decouple from local spiking activity.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 13 | Spike-LFP Decoupling | Oscillations occur without corresponding local unit firing activity. | Empirical |
| 14 | Sub-threshold Resonance | Oscillations driven by intrinsic membrane properties (e.g. M-current). | Biophysical |
| 15 | Volumetric Conduction | Signals originating from distant areas detected via passive conduction. | Methodological |
| 16 | Glial Modulation | Astrocytic contributions to slow-wave oscillatory dynamics. | Biological |
| 17 | Ephaptic Coupling | Local field gradients influencing spike timing via non-synaptic drive. | Biophysical |
| 18 | Dendritic-Somatic Offset | Oscillations driven by dendritic currents that are soma-invisible. | Biophysical |
| 19 | The Slaving Principle | Slow-evolving electric fields enslave fast-evolving neuron states. | Theoretical |
| 20 | Slow-Control Parameter | Fields act as governors that constrain order parameters of activity. | Theoretical |
| 21 | Cytoelectric Coupling | Fields "tune" the brain infrastructure including the cytoskeleton. | Hypothesis |
| 22 | Field-Mediated Synchrony | Spiking synchrony achieved without direct synaptic recurrence. | Modeling |
| 23 | Quasi-Static Field State | The assumption that tissue reactance is negligible in field propagation. | Physics |
| 24 | Mesoscale Energy Transfer | Transformation between electrical potentials and metabolic energy. | Biophysical |

Evaluates how oscillations interact across scales and hierarchical levels.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |

| 27 | Traveling Wave Propagation | Synchronous wavefronts advancing sequentially across the cortex. | Neurophys |

| 29 | Phase-Amplitude Modulation | Direct information encoding in the phase-amplitude manifold. | Functional |

| 32 | Distractor Pathway Segregation | Waves route memory and distractors along distinct sequences. | Functional |
| 33 | Spatial Computing | Routing of item-specific representations across network space. | Theoretical |
| 34 | Weight-to-Field Correlation | Synaptic conductances correlating with trial-by-trial field power. | Empirical |
| 35 | Wave-Based Computation | Analog spatiotemporal processing via propagating wave packets. | Computational |
| 36 | Cross-Trial Power Variability | Variability in power reflecting functional cytoelectric tuning. | Functional |
