# Electromagnetic Oscillations & Brain Signals (EMO-36) -- Glossary Reference

This document defines the 36 biophysical factors for evaluating theories of brain oscillations and signal modulation.

---

## H1: Functional Role of Oscillations (Agree/Disagree [-1 to +1])
Evaluates if oscillations are causal functional units or epiphenomena.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Scale |
|----|-------------|------------|-------|
| 1 | Causal Pacing | Oscillations actively gate and coordinate neuronal firing. | -1 to 1 |
| 2 | Information Routing | Specific frequencies (e.g. Gamma) route signals across areas. | -1 to 1 |
| 3 | Temporal Binding | Synchrony binds disparate sensory features into objects. | -1 to 1 |
| 4 | Working Memory Buffering | Theta-Gamma coupling maintains items in active memory. | -1 to 1 |
| 5 | Phase-Reset Signaling | Event-related phase resets convey sensory timing. | -1 to 1 |
| 6 | Metabolic Efficiency | Oscillations optimize the energy cost of spike transmission. | -1 to 1 |

---

## H2: Independence from Spiking
Evaluates the degree to which LFP/MEG signals decouple from local spiking activity.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition |
|----|-------------|------------|
| 13 | Spike-LFP Decoupling | Oscillations occur without corresponding local unit firing. |
| 14 | Sub-threshold Resonance | Oscillations driven by intrinsic membrane properties (M-current). |
| 15 | Volumetric Conduction | Signals originating from distant areas via volume conduction. |
| 16 | Glial Modulation | Non-neuronal (astrocytic) contributions to slow oscillations. |
| 17 | Ephaptic Coupling | Local field gradients influencing spike timing directly. |
| 18 | Dendritic-Somatic Offset | Oscillations driven by dendritic currents invisible at the soma. |

---

## H3: Signal Modulation & Hierarchy
Evaluates how oscillations interact across scales and hierarchical levels.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition |
|----|-------------|------------|
| 25 | Cross-Frequency Coupling | Low-frequency phase modulating high-frequency amplitude. |
| 26 | Hierarchical Gain Control | Top-down Alpha/Beta modulating bottom-up Gamma gain. |
| 27 | Traveling Wave Propagation | Spatial movement of oscillatory phases across the cortex. |
| 28 | Aperiodic (1/f) Shift | Modulations in the non-oscillatory power spectrum slope. |
| 29 | Phase-Amplitude Modulation | Direct encoding of information in the phase-amplitude space. |
| 30 | Area-Specific Resonance | Natural resonant frequencies defining area hierarchy (e.g. V1 > PFC). |
