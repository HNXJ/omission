# Schizophrenia Biophysics Factors (ScZ-40) -- Glossary Reference

This document defines the 40 biophysical factors used to evaluate the cellular basis of Schizophrenia, organized by hypothesis group.

---

## H1: The GABAergic & Interneuron Insult (IDs 1--14)
Mechanisms focusing on the failure of inhibitory microcircuits to pace and gate cortical activity.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 1 | PV mRNA Reduction | Significant decrease in Parvalbumin mRNA expression in L2/3. | Quantitative |
| 2 | GAD67 Deficit | Reduction in the 67kDa isoform of glutamic acid decarboxylase. | Quantitative |
| 3 | Fast-Spiking Failure | Inability of PV interneurons to maintain high-frequency (>40Hz) pacing. | Biophysical |
| 4 | SST mRNA Reduction | Decrease in Somatostatin expression, primarily in superficial layers. | Quantitative |
| 5 | Perisomatic Shunting Failure | Weakened PV-mediated shunting inhibition at the pyramidal soma. | Biophysical |
| 6 | Dendritic Gating Failure | Weakened SST-mediated inhibition of distal dendritic feedback. | Biophysical |
| 7 | CCK-Interneuron Dysregulation | Altered activity of Cholecystokinin-positive basket cells. | Qualitative |
| 8 | L3 PV Density Shift | Anatomical reduction of PV+ cell counts specifically in Layer 3. | Anatomical |
| 9 | GAT-1 Downregulation | Reduced GABA transporter-1 expression at inhibitory terminals. | Quantitative |
| 10 | GABA-A Receptor $\alpha$1 Subunit Shift | Compensatory changes in postsynaptic GABA-A receptor composition. | Quantitative |
| 11 | Synchrony Pacing Deficit | Failure of interneuron networks to drive coherent population oscillations. | Functional |
| 12 | PV-Pyr Synaptic Weakening | Reduced maximal conductance ($g_{max}$) of PV $\to$ E synapses. | Biophysical |
| 13 | SST-Pyr Synaptic Weakening | Reduced maximal conductance ($g_{max}$) of SST $\to$ E synapses. | Biophysical |
| 14 | E/I Ratio Volatility | Increased baseline fluctuations in the local excitation/inhibition balance. | Functional |

## H2: Structural & Excitatory Dysregulation (IDs 15--28)
Focuses on NMDA hypofunction, synaptic pruning, and long-range disconnection.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 15 | NMDAR Hypofunction (PV) | Reduced NMDA receptor activity specifically on PV+ interneurons. | Biophysical |
| 16 | GluN1 Subunit Deficit | Global or regional reduction in the essential NMDA receptor subunit. | Quantitative |
| 17 | L3 Pyramidal Spine Loss | Significant reduction in dendritic spine density on L3 excitatory cells. | Anatomical |
| 18 | Thalamocortical Weakening | Reduced excitatory drive from thalamic nuclei to Layer 4. | Functional |
| 19 | Cortical Hypo-dopaminergia | Reduced D1 receptor stimulation in the Prefrontal Cortex. | Neurochemical |
| 20 | Subcortical Hyper-dopaminergia | Excessive D2 receptor activity in the Striatum. | Neurochemical |
| 21 | AMPA/NMDA Ratio Shift | Altered balance of fast vs. slow excitatory neurotransmission. | Quantitative |
| 22 | Recurrent E-E Weakening | Reduced strength of local excitatory-to-excitatory connections. | Biophysical |
| 23 | White Matter Disintegrity | Disruption of long-range axonal tracts (e.g., Uncinate Fasciculus). | Structural |
| 24 | Dendritic Pruning Over-activity | Pathological levels of microglia-mediated synaptic elimination. | Biological |
| 25 | Axon Initial Segment (AIS) Shift | Altered length or position of the AIS in Pyramidal neurons. | Anatomical |
| 26 | GluN2A/2B Imbalance | Shift in the ratio of NMDA receptor kinetic subunits. | Quantitative |
| 27 | KCC2/NKCC1 Ratio Shift | Altered chloride homeostasis affecting GABAergic polarity. | Quantitative |
| 28 | BDNF Expression Deficit | Reduced Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor levels affecting plasticity. | Quantitative |

## H3: Functional Signatures & Predictive Failure (IDs 29--40)
The macroscopic consequences of microcircuit failure on oscillations and inference.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|
| 29 | Reduced Task-Evoked Gamma | Failure to increase 30-80Hz power during cognitive/sensory load. | Biomarker |
| 30 | Enhanced Baseline Beta | Pathologically elevated 15-25Hz power during rest or pre-stimulus. | Biomarker |
| 31 | MMN Blunting | Reduced amplitude of the Mismatch Negativity ERP to oddballs. | Functional |
| 32 | ASSR Deficit | Impaired Auditory Steady-State Response to 40Hz click trains. | Functional |
| 33 | 1/f Slope Flattening | Reduced slope of the aperiodic power spectrum (Disinhibition signature). | Biomarker |
| 34 | Variability Quenching Failure | Lack of trial-by-trial variance reduction following stimulus onset. | Biomarker |
| 35 | Prediction Error Attenuation | Reduced neural differentiation between expected and unexpected events. | Cognitive |
| 36 | Hyper-sensitive Sensory Gating | Failure to filter redundant sensory information (P50 deficit). | Functional |
| 37 | Theta-Gamma Coupling Deficit | Disrupted phase-amplitude coupling between low and high frequencies. | Biomarker |
| 38 | Long-Range Phase Coherence Drop | Reduced synchronization between distant cortical areas (e.g., V1-PFC). | Functional |
| 39 | Aperiodic Noise Volatility | Increased temporal fluctuations in non-oscillatory background activity. | Biomarker |
| 40 | Delta/Theta Baseline Surge | Pathological increase in low-frequency power during wakefulness. | Biomarker |
