# Manuscript Writing & Figure-First Strategy

We adhere to a 'Figure-First' mandate, where the structure of the paper is built around the core data visualizations.

Manuscript Sections:
1. Abstract: Focused on the divergence of V1-PFC latencies during omissions as evidence for Active Inference.
2. Methods: Detailed descriptions of NWB alignment, ternary decoding, and MMFF calculations.
3. Results:
   - Figure 1: Pupil/Eye Identity Decoding.
   - Figure 2: Surprise Latency Hierarchy.
   - Figure 3: V1-PFC Effective Connectivity (Granger).
   - Figure 4: Variability Quenching (MMFF).
4. Discussion: Reconciliation of results with Friston's Free Energy Principle and biological predictive coding.

PLOS ONE Rebuttal Strategy:
Focus on the robustness of the Mean-Matched Fano Factor to control for firing rate artifacts and the rigorous cross-validation of SVM decoders.

Reference Standards:
All citations must follow the DOI format. All units must be SI standard.

Writing Style:
- Professional, concise, and technical.
- Use 'We observe...' or 'The data suggests...' to maintain objective distance.
- Ensure all symbols (e.g., Gamma, Theta, D_KL) are rendered correctly in LaTeX.

References:
1. Bem, D. J. (2003). Writing the Empirical Journal Article.
2. Mensh, B., & Kording, K. (2017). Ten simple rules for structuring papers. PLOS Computational Biology.
