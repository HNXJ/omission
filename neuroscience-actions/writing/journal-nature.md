# Neuroscience Writing: Nature Communications Standards

Specific mandates for Nature Portfolio submissions.

## 1. Article Specifications
- **Abstract**: 150 words maximum, unreferenced.
- **Main Text**: 5,000 – 6,500 words (Intro, Results, Discussion).
- **Methods**: Placed after the Discussion. No word limit, but must be technical and concise.
- **Display Items**: 6 to 10 figures or tables.

## 2. Structural Requirements
- **Introduction**: No subheadings.
- **Results**: Use descriptive subheadings for each major finding.
- **Discussion**: Synthesize findings without repeating results.
- **References**: Numbered citations (e.g., [1]) in order of appearance. Limit to ~70.

## 3. High-Impact Narrative
- Focus on "Mechanistic Insight": Nature Communications favors papers that explain *how* a process works (e.g., the specific biophysical role of PV cells in PC).
- Boldly state the "Novelty": How does this AI meta-analysis change our understanding of Predictive Coding?
