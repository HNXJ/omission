# Introduction: The Quest for Predictive Coding Consensus

## 1. The Context
Predictive Coding (PC) has emerged as the leading framework for understanding brain function, positing that the brain is an inference machine constantly minimizing prediction errors.

## 2. The Problem (The PC Gap)
Despite its theoretical dominance, empirical evidence for PC is scattered across thousands of papers using diverse species (macaque, human, mouse), signals (LFP, MEG, Spike), and paradigms (Local-Global Oddball, Omission). Traditional human-led meta-analyses are vulnerable to:
- **Selection Bias**: Favoring papers that match the reviewer's intuition.
- **Dimensionality Limits**: Struggling to track 30+ biophysical factors simultaneously across hundreds of studies.

## 3. Our Solution: Multi-Agent AI Synthesis
We present a novel, AI-driven meta-analytical framework. By deploying an ensemble of Large Language Models (LLMs) to score 200+ studies against the unified TcGLO Glossary, we quantify the field's consensus. This approach allows us to map the "Certainty Landscape" of Predictive Coding with unprecedented resolution.
