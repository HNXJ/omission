# Results: Phase 2 - Oscillatory Coordination

## 📊 Omission-Locked Gamma Bursts and Beta Suppression
We next examined the oscillatory dynamics underlying the omission response. TFR analysis revealed two distinct spectral signatures following the omission event (FIG_05). First, we observed a transient increase in gamma-band power (40-80Hz), which was most pronounced in superficial layers. Second, we observed a sustained suppression of beta-band power (15-25Hz) across all layers. These dynamics are consistent with the "Predictive Routing" model, where gamma oscillations signal the occurrence of an unpredicted error, and beta suppression reflects the updating of internal predictions.

## 📊 Hierarchical Gradient of Spectral Modulation
The magnitude of both gamma enhancement and beta suppression showed a significant hierarchical gradient. Using band-specific power manifolds, we quantified that high-order areas (PFC, FEF) exhibited >3dB stronger gamma bursts compared to early visual areas (V1, V2) ($p < 1e-5$, FIG_06). This suggests that the "neural surprise" signal is progressively amplified as it propagates through the cortical hierarchy.

## 📊 Spiking-Oscillatory Coupling (SFC)
To determine if these global oscillations directly modulate local neural processing, we calculated the Spike-Field Coherence (SFC) using the PPC metric. We found a significant increase in gamma-band phase-locking during the omission window across all hierarchical levels (FIG_07). Units showed a preferred spiking phase relative to the local gamma rhythm, indicating that the omission response is not merely a change in firing rate but a fundamental shift in the temporal coordination of neural ensembles.
