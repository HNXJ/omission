# FIG_01: Predictive Routing Theory & Omission Logic

## 🎯 Intent
To establish the conceptual framework of "Predictive Routing," where feedback (Deep Layers -> Superficial Layers) carries internal predictions and feedforward (Superficial Layers -> Deep Layers) carries prediction errors (omissions).

## 🔬 Methodology
- **Source**: `src/f001_theory/plot.py`
- **Logic**: Schematic representation of the V1-PFC axis.
- **Layers**: Stratified into Superficial (L2/3) and Deep (L5/6).
- **Signal Flow**: 
    - Gamma (Feedforward)
    - Alpha/Beta (Feedback)

## 📊 Observations
- Baseline assumption: Standard trials exhibit stereotypical stimulus-driven activity.
- Omission assumption: Absence of expected input triggers a distinct "ghost signal" in feedforward channels.
