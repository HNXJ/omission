# Plan: Multi-Scale Omission Dynamics (LFP Power + Eye Precision)

## 🎯 Objective
Quantify the neural and behavioral fingerprints of the "Ghost Signal" by analyzing the relative change in LFP spectral power and oculomotor precision during the 1531ms window surrounding an omission.

## 🔬 Implementation Steps

### 1. LFP Relative Power Change (TFR-dB)
- **Windowing**: For any omission `p-k` (where $k \in \{2, 3, 4\}$), extract the full serial window from `d-(k-1)` to `d-k` (~1531ms).
- **TFR Parameters**:
    - **Window Size (w)**: 100ms.
    - **Overlap**: 98% (2ms step).
    - **Window Type**: Hanning.
    - **Smoothing**: 2D Gaussian width of 20ms applied post-calculation.
- **Normalization (Relative Change)**:
    - **Baseline**: Average power per frequency band during the delays `d-(k-1)` and `d-k` within the same trial.
    - **Metric**: $10 \times \log_{10}(P_{time} / P_{baseline})$.
- **Aggregation**: Plot trial-averaged traces with $\pm$SEM shaded patches across areas, layers, and conditions.

### 2. Oculomotor Precision Analysis
- **Source**: Extract high-resolution eye trajectories (X, Y) from behavioral `.mat` files or the `.npy` behavioral extractions.
- **Metrics**:
    - **XY Variance**: Temporal variance of eye position within the 1531ms window.
    - **Microsaccades**: Detect events using velocity thresholds (>30°/s) and analyze frequency/amplitude during omissions.
    - **Jittering**: Quantify high-frequency micro-oscillations in eye position.
- **Hypothesis**: Active inference predicts a "precision scaling" effect where eye stabilization improves (quenching) after an omission.

### 3. Key Files & Context
- **Script**: `scripts/run_omission_dynamics_lfp_eye.py` (New).
- **Functions**: `functions/neuro_variability_suite.py` (for quenching metrics).
- **Data**: `data/ses*-lfp-*.npy` and `data/ses*-behavioral-*.npy`.
